Dispersed Camping

🎒 Gear List

BLM and NF dispersed sites have zero infrastructure — no water, no toilets, no fire rings, no waste disposal. Everything you need, you carry in and out. Tent camping is 100% allowed (no vehicle required). Below is what you need for this specific trip.

Tent camping on BLM / NF dispersed — yes, 100% allowed. No RV or roof tent required. Set up wherever the terrain allows, at least 200 ft from water sources and trails. 14-night limit per spot. Leave No Trace — pack out everything.
💧 Water
40L capacity minimum for 3 people + 2 dogs
No water at any dispersed site. Fill at every town before heading out. Gear: 2× 20L jerry cans or large water jugs, 1L Nalgene per person for hiking, 1× Sawyer Squeeze filter (emergencies), iodine tablets as last resort. Dogs: 1–2L each per day in August heat. Never let dogs drink from streams (Giardia).
🏕 Shelter
3-season tent · sleeping bags (rated 0°C) · pads · ground tarp
Mammoth Lakes and Tonopah can drop to 5–8°C at night even in August — don't underestimate elevation. Sleeping pad R-value ≥ 2. Ground tarp/footprint under tent to prevent moisture. Extra tent stakes (desert ground can be hard, winds at Grand Staircase can be strong). Seam-seal your tent before leaving — monsoon storms are real. Tent repair kit (patches, seam sealer).
🔥 Cooking
Camp stove · 4 fuel canisters · pot set · cooler · utensils
Fire bans are almost guaranteed in August in AZ, UT, NV — never rely on open fire. Jetboil or MSR PocketRocket. Bring: 2L pot + lid, frying pan, 3× camp mugs, 3× sporks, folding knife, cutting board, can opener, lighter × 2. Cooler (hard-sided if possible): fill with block ice every 2–3 days at gas stations. Ziplock bags for leftovers. Biodegradable soap (Dr. Bronner's) + sponge. 3× large garbage bags. Cooler stays in car or in shade — never in direct sun.
🥫 Food Planning
Plan meals for 3–4 days ahead at all times
Long stretches with no shops: Valley of Gods → Monument Valley → Flagstaff (buy in Bluff or Mexican Hat), Tonopah → Grand Staircase (buy in Cedar City), Grand Staircase → Mancos (buy in Escalante or Torrey). Stock up: instant oats, pasta, rice, canned beans, peanut butter, tortillas, hard cheese, dried fruit, nuts, energy bars, instant coffee. Fresh food lasts 2–3 days in a good cooler. Bear canister required at Mammoth + Grand Staircase — pack all food inside.
🗺 Navigation
Gaia GPS (offline BLM maps) · Campendium · iOverlander · paper map
Download offline maps and BLM boundary layers before leaving — zero cell signal at Valley of Gods, Tonopah BLM, Grand Staircase, Los Padres NF, and more. Save GPS coordinates of each planned camp. Campendium and iOverlander: community reviews with road conditions and exact coordinates. Physical AAA Southwest map as hard backup. Note: Google Maps is useless without cell signal — Gaia GPS is mandatory.
📡 Emergency
Satellite communicator (Garmin inReach Mini) · first aid kit · emergency blanket
No cell signal at multiple stops — the inReach lets you send an SOS or message anyone from anywhere on earth. First aid kit contents: adhesive bandages (various sizes), Moleskin (blisters), gauze + medical tape, tweezers (cactus spines, splinters), SAM splint, Ace bandage, ibuprofen + paracetamol, antihistamine (Benadryl), Imodium (stomach upset), antiseptic wipes, blister treatment (Compeed), emergency blanket × 2. Whistle. Lighter. Written emergency contact list (paper, not just in phone).
🚿 Shower & Toilet
No toilets at dispersed sites — ever. Pop-up shower tent + solar shower bag.
Toilet: Sturdy trowel (Deuce of Spades or similar) — desert ground is rock-hard. Cathole: 15–20 cm deep, 60m from water/trails/camp. Toilet paper in paper bag → pack out. Hand sanitizer immediately after. WAG bags as alternative (pack-out bags, mandatory in some areas).
Shower: Pop-up privacy tent (~$30, folds to a disc) + 20L solar shower bag (leave in sun 2–3h for warm water). Biodegradable soap. Microfiber towels × 3. Alternatively: Planet Fitness day pass ($10–15): Flagstaff ✅ · Thousand Oaks (Day 3–5, ~30 min from Los Padres via Ojai) ✅ · Cedar City (Day 9) ✅. Mammoth Lakes: Shady Rest Rec Center ($8–10). Tonopah: Jim Butler Motel or truck stop shower (~$10).
👕 Clothing
Layers for 5°C nights and 38°C days — same trip
Per person: 2× lightweight hiking pants/shorts, 2× long-sleeve UV protection shirts (UPF 50+), 1× fleece or down jacket (mandatory for Mammoth/Tonopah evenings), 1× hardshell rain jacket, 3× merino wool socks (dry fast, no blisters), underwear × 4, warm beanie (nights at elevation), wide-brim sun hat, buff/neck gaiter (dust), hiking boots or trail runners (worn in!), camp sandals (Crocs/Birkenstock). Swimwear for Horseshoe Lake (Mammoth) and Wild Willy's Hot Springs.
🔦 Lighting & Power
Headlamps × 3 · power bank 20,000 mAh · 12V car charger · camp lantern
Headlamp (Black Diamond Spot or similar) per person + spare batteries. Camp lantern (LED, rechargeable) for cooking and evenings. Power bank: 20,000+ mAh covers 2 full phone charges each. 12V car charger: charge power banks + devices while driving. Optional: 60W folding solar panel — useful for 3-night stretches like Grand Staircase. No power outlets at any dispersed site.
☀️ Sun & Heat
SPF 50+ sunscreen · UV shirt · wide-brim hat · shade tarp
High altitude + desert = brutal UV. Valley of Gods, Rainbow Basin, Tonopah, Grand Staircase all exceed 35°C midday. Hike before 09:00 or after 17:30 without exception. Test asphalt with your bare hand before letting dogs walk on it — paw burns happen within seconds. Shade tarp (3×3m, with poles) for midday camp shade. Cooling towels for dogs. Never leave dogs or food in parked car.
🌧 Rain & Flash Floods
Rain jacket · seam-sealed tent · Rain Alarm app
August = monsoon season in AZ and UT. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, intense, and fast. Flash flood rule: never camp in a wash, canyon bottom, or dry riverbed — they fill in minutes from storms miles away. Camp on high ground. Check radar every morning (Rain Alarm or Weather Underground). If thunder: off ridge tops and summits immediately. All gear in waterproof bags or dry bags when not in use.
🐻 Bears
Bear canister (BearVault BV500) required at Mammoth + Grand Staircase
Dog food = food. Toothpaste, sunscreen, deodorant, lip balm = scented items. All must go into the bear canister or locked car boot — never in the tent, never on the picnic table. BearVault BV500 holds ~4 days of food for 2 people. At Los Padres NF (Ventura back-country): black bears present, same rules apply. Hang food in a tree if no canister at non-required areas.
🐾 Dogs
Dog food (in bear canister) · 2× collapsible bowl · 2× leashes · dog first aid kit · paw care
Dog first aid kit: vet wrap, antiseptic wipes, tweezers (cactus thorns), eye wash, small cone (Elizabethan collar), metronidazole tablets (Giardia — get prescription from vet before trip). Paw care: coconut oil or butter (removes tar), paw balm (cracked pads from hot rock), booties optional. Dog sleeping pads for cold nights (Mammoth, Tonopah). Cooling mat or wet towel for car stops. Flea/tick treatment applied before trip (Nexgard or Frontline). Cooling vest for hot hiking. Always carry leash even on BLM — be ready instantly.
🪑 Comfort
Camp chairs × 3 · folding table · shade tarp with poles
You'll spend a lot of time at camp. Lightweight camp chairs (Helinox or similar). Small folding table for cooking. 3×3m tarp on trekking poles or bungee lines = shade shelter for midday. Hammock if space allows (great in Los Padres NF trees). Duct tape (fixes everything). Rope/paracord (tarp lines, hanging food).
🚗 Vehicle
Full tank before every BLM road · spare tire (inflated!) · recovery kit
Valley of Gods: 17 km dirt road (2WD OK when dry). Rainbow Basin: gravel, fine. Grand Staircase Hole-in-the-Rock Road: sandy sections possible. Los Padres CA-33: paved but winding mountain road. Fill gas in last town before any dirt road. Recovery kit: tire plug kit + 12V air compressor, jumper cables, tow strap, small shovel, WD-40. Basic toolkit (pliers, screwdrivers, zip ties). Road flares or reflective triangles. Note tire pressure recommended for your vehicle — deflate slightly for sand.
🧴 Hygiene
Biodegradable soap only · wet wipes · hand sanitizer · microfiber towels
Regular soap pollutes water sources. Dr. Bronner's castile soap works for body, dishes, and laundry. Wet wipes (biodegradable) for quick clean-ups. Microfiber towels × 3 (pack small, dry fast). Hand sanitizer before every meal — always. Dental hygiene: spit toothpaste 60m from water. Store all scented items (toothpaste, deodorant, sunscreen) in bear canister at Mammoth/Grand Staircase. Small mirror for splinter removal.
🔥 Fire Rules
Check fire restrictions daily — August = high fire danger throughout the Southwest
Check: fs.usda.gov/alerts (NF areas) and blm.gov/programs/fire (BLM areas) each morning. Fire bans are common and fines are serious. If fires allowed: use only established fire rings or a fire pan (Leave No Trace). Never leave fire unattended. Drown with water, stir, drown again — cold to the touch before sleeping. Camp stove is your primary cooking method regardless. Fire extinguisher in the car.
📄 Documents
ID · insurance · America the Beautiful Pass · emergency contacts (paper)
America the Beautiful Annual Pass ($80) covers all NPS day-use fees (Capitol Reef, Grand Canyon overlook, etc.) — worth it for this trip. BLM and NF dispersed: no permit needed. Dog vaccination records (some NPS areas require proof at trailhead). Vet's emergency phone number. Written list of emergency contacts — do not rely only on your phone. Roadside assistance number (AAA or equivalent). Download all reservations offline (hotel, Warner Bros. etc.).
